Data collection/curation + parsing¶

We go to the GitHub provided in rubric and download the dataset Titanic.csv. This excel has many columns, including PassengerId, name, age and other information. PassengerId, Name: This information has no effect on survival from common sense. (I know there may have some royal name and some title may show something. But, I assume noble status will be reflected in the cabin now) Ticket: It looks messy and should not be used in data mining Cabin: Too many Null value in it. Drop this column. Pclass, Fare: Fare is closely related to Pclass. We only use Pclass to show where the customer is. Sex: Lady first. Important column. Age: Obviously Important. Parch, SibSp, Embarked: hard to say. It may be analyzed from the Family size and boarding city.

Exploratory data analysis¶

First, we cross analyzed Pclass and survival. We suspect that different classes may affect the survival rate. We count the number of survivors and deaths in each Pclass. The bar graph is drawn by pyplot:

In [6]:
class_survived=titanic[['Pclass','Survived']]
y_live=class_survived[class_survived.Survived==1].groupby('Pclass')['Survived'].count()
y_dead=class_survived[class_survived.Survived==0].groupby('Pclass')['Survived'].count()
pos=range(1,4)
defination=['class%d'%(i) for i in range(1,4)]
plt.bar(pos,y_live,label='survived')
plt.bar(pos,y_dead,bottom=y_live,label='not survived')
plt.legend()
plt.xticks(pos,defination)
plt.title('Pclass Surveved count')
plt.show()

We can clearly see that the survival rate of first class is higher than that of second class, and second class is much higher than that of third class. Our inference was confirmed by statistics.

Next, we suspect the survival rate may be related to gender. We count the number of survivors and deaths in male and female:We cross analyzed gender and survival

In [7]:
sex_survived=titanic[['Sex','Survived']]
y_male=[0,0] #live/die
y_female=[0,0]#live/die
for i in range(len(sex_survived.Sex)):
    if sex_survived.Sex[i]:#male = 1, famale = 0
        if sex_survived.Survived[i]==0:
            y_male[0]+=1
        else:
            y_male[1]+=1
    else:
        if sex_survived.Survived[i]==0:
            y_female[0]+=1
        else:
            y_female[1]+=1
x_number=range(1,3)
x_word=['survived','not survived']
plt.bar(x_number,y_male,label='female')
plt.bar(x_number,y_female,bottom=y_male,label='male')
plt.legend()
plt.xticks(x_number,x_word)
plt.show()

Yes, we can see that most of the people who survived were male, while most of the people who died were female. The survival rate of female is more than three-quarters, while that of male is less than half. Our inference was confirmed by statistics. ‘Lady first’was indeed realized at that time.

def splitTitle(name):
    temp = name.split(',')[1].split('.')[0]
    temp1 = ''.join(temp)
    return temp1

titleDF=pd.DataFrame()
titleDF['Title']=titanic['Name'].map(splitTitle)

We can see that there are too many titles. We need to simplify them and classify them into several categories. Through discussion, we divided all titles into six categories:

(1) Officer: government personnel (soldier + priest). (2) Royalty: royal family. (3) MR: adult man. (4) MRS: married woman. (5) Miss: young unmarried woman. (6) Master: mechanic, teacher

And we mapped all Title into these six different titles and compared their survival rates.
